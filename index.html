About Astronaut

The most basic education related to this field of work is a bachelor's degree in engineering, biology, physical science, or mathematics, which is required for the position. Other requirements are having prior experience related to this field of work and a minimum of 1,000 hours of pilot-flying time.

Not A One Man Team

Going to space is a team mission, which means it will require many people. Examples of people who are needed include the flight director, who is in charge of the overall shuttle mission. A ground controller is similar, as they are under the command of the MCC and direct maintenance and operation activities. Another one is the spacecraft communicator, who is the link between flight control and astronauts.

Getting The Body Ready

In order to qualify as an astronaut, you will need up to two years of training. You must know the basics of the Space Shuttle and the International Space Station. You must also know how to fly a jet. Along with knowing basic sciences, astronauts need to know medical procedures and be taking survival courses to be prepared for any emergency.
